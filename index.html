FUTMinna Leads the Charge in Revolutionising Academic Publishing in Nigeria

The Federal University of Technology, Minna (FUTMinna), spearheads a transformative initiative to revolutionise academic book publishing in Nigeria. Rooted in the vision of democratising access to knowledge and fostering sustainable publishing ecosystems, this movement began at the workshop for the LIBSENSE in Nigeria Book Project, which catalysed discussions on leveraging Open Access (OA) and digital tools to modernise academic book publishing.

Through strategic partnerships with Datasphir and funding support from the Open Book Collective (OBC), FUTMinna is turning these discussions into action, setting a precedent for innovation in Nigerian academia and beyond.

Professor Abdulsalami Kovo

Professor Abdulsalami Kovo is a distinguished faculty member in the Chemical Engineering department at the Federal University of Technology, Minna (FUTMinna) in Nigeria. Holding a PhD from the University of Manchester, his research interests encompass sustainable chemical processes, renewable energy solutions, and innovative publishing practices in academia.

Dr. Fatimah Jibril Abduldayan

An insightful analyst and incurable go-getter, Dr. Fatimah is committed to using sophisticated quantitative and qualitative data analysis tools and methods to guide the conduct of original, transparent, reusable, and development-based research. As the Head of the Department of Library and Information Science at the Federal University of Technology, Minna (FUTMinna), she is also the Founder of Maternal Fortify and a dedicated Lecturer at FUTMinna. Her past experiences include significant contributions to the Nigerian Library Association (NLA).
