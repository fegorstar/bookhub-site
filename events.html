About the Event

The "Revolutionizing Academic Publishing in Nigeria" event will focus on the future of Open Access (OA) publishing in Nigerian universities. We aim to showcase how Nigerian academia can take charge of producing, sharing, and publishing high-quality academic content through OA platforms. This is an opportunity for professionals, academics, and stakeholders to engage in meaningful discussions about shaping the future of academic publishing in Nigeria.

Prof. Faruk Adamu Kuta

Professor Faruk Adamu Kuta is the Vice-Chancellor of the Federal University of Technology Minna (FUTMinna). Born on October 26, 1972, in Kuta, Shiroro Local Government Area, Niger State, he has held various academic and administrative positions within the university. Prof. Kuta attended Government Science Teachers College Wushishi and the Federal College of Education, Kontangora, before earning his PhD in Medical Microbiology from Abubakar Tafawa Balewa University, Bauchi. Prior to his appointment as Vice-Chancellor, he served as Deputy Vice-Chancellor (Academic) at FUTMinna.

Professor Abdulsalami Kovo

Professor Abdulsalami Kovo is a distinguished academic in Chemical Engineering and currently serves as the Director of the Academic Publishing Centre (APC) at the Federal University of Technology Minna (FUTMinna). He earned his PhD in Chemical Engineering and has contributed significantly to research in sustainable chemical processes and renewable energy solutions. Prof. Kovo is also involved in various initiatives aimed at enhancing academic publishing practices within the university and beyond.

×

Dr. Fatimah Jibril Abduldayan

Dr. Fatimah Jibril Abduldayan is the Head of the Department of Library and Information Science at the Federal University of Technology Minna (FUTMinna). She is also the Team Lead for LIBSENSE Nigeria, advocating for open access and research data management in Nigeria. Dr. Abduldayan has been instrumental in leading nationwide initiatives to enhance research data management, open access publishing, and research assessment reforms. She actively contributes to policy development, capacity building, and sustainable publishing efforts within the academic community.
